REFUGEES

Working tirelessly for the rights
and well-being of immigrants and refugees.


Situation of Refugees

More than 110 million people are forcibly displaced from their homes due to wars, violence, and serious violations of their fundamental rights. This represents more than 1% of the world’s population and the highest number ever recorded by UNHCR. These figures speak for themselves about the refugee situation.

Millions of refugees have been forced to flee their countries to save their lives.
They flee from persecution due to race, religion, nationality, political opinions, gender, or sexual orientation.
At the end of 2023, according to UNHCR data, more than 43 million people had crossed borders seeking protection, and 68 million more were internally displaced within their own countries.
From our NGO, we invite you to be part of the change: collaborate, support, or join as a volunteer to offer hope and dignity to those who need it most.

Guediouma fled the war in Mali

The war changed Guediouma's life forever.
At a very young age, he had to make the decision to flee to live in peace without fear for his life, even though it meant leaving everything behind. “I didn’t feel safe in my country, that’s why I decided to leave.”

Yolanda and William: having to flee in their 60s

An Ecuadorian couple had to flee their country after suffering threats and extortion, despite living a stable life. The decision to flee was not easy.
Today they are safe in a shelter, though they face the challenge of starting over at an older age and uncertainty about their future employment.

Maria’s wish for her country: “I hope the bullets stop”

A young woman from San Salvador had to flee with her brother to escape the violence at home, marked by the presence of a dangerous gang.
Today, after years of fear, she has found a place in Spain where she can start over in peace.
